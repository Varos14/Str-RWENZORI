About Our Community Organization

Save the Rwenzori is a registered Community-Based Organization (CBO) in Bundibugyo District (Registration No: BD/CBO/2023/0456), founded by local residents to protect our mountain ecosystem. We are community volunteers working to preserve our natural heritage for future generations.

Our Community Approach

  • 100% locally-led initiatives
  • All programs designed with community input through village meetings
  • 90% of staff are Bundibugyo residents
  • Decisions made by local advisory council
  • Working in 12 sub-counties across Bundibugyo District

The Crisis in Our Bundibugyo

Mountains of the Moon in Peril

The Rwenzori Mountains, known locally as "Rwenzururu," are facing an unprecedented environmental crisis that threatens our communities in Bundibugyo District.

Key Threats to Our Region:

  • Glacial Retreat: Over 60% of glaciers have disappeared since 1900, with complete loss projected by 2030
  • Landslides & Floods: Steep slopes and intense rainfall cause devastating landslides like the 2020 disaster in Bupomboli that killed 100+ people
  • Deforestation: 30% forest cover loss since 1990 due to charcoal production in areas like Bubukwanga
  • Biodiversity Loss: Habitat destruction threatens endemic species like the Rwenzori duiker
  • Agricultural Collapse: Coffee and cocoa crops failing due to erratic rainfall in Busaru and Harugale

Our Community Solutions

Locally-developed initiatives to protect Bundibugyo's environment

Community Reforestation

Planting native trees with schools and farmers in Bubukwanga, Busaru and Ntotoro

$5 plants a tree with a local farmer

Impact: Restores watersheds, creates wildlife corridors

Beehive Fences

Installing beehive barriers to protect farms in Kasitu and Ndugutu from elephants

$70 builds one fence with a local beekeeper

Impact: Reduces human-wildlife conflict, creates honey income

Landslide Early Warning

Installing rain gauges in Bupomboli and Bughendera to provide life-saving alerts

$150 protects one village

Impact: Saves lives, protects homes and farmland
